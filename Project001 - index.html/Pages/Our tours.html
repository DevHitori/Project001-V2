Port of Spain

Port of Spain, is the capital of Trinidad and Tobago since 1757. It is located near the northern ranges of the island. Long-ago a mangrove swampland occupied the area, now the area is a beautiful waterfront, part of which is used as a regional shipping hub.Now it is a commercially busy area filled with large corporations, financial institutes, home to the largest banks in the English speaking Caribbean, beautiful luxury hotels etc.The heart of the city holds the Queens park savanna, Port of Spain's largest open space( 260 acres of level land).

Maracus Beach

Maracus beach, home of the famous traditional meal bake and shark, is located on the nothern side of the island. It is one of the most popular beaches on the island with it's golden sand and numerous tall palm trees. On your way through the mountainous scenic drive, you can enjoy the Maracus lookout, where you can take pictures of the sea and enjoy local snacks.

Las Cuevas Beach

This peaceful and charming beach has been known to provide the perfect escape. It is about a 15-minute drive east of Maracas on the north coast road. Come and enjoy the caves located on the southwestern end of Las Cuevas beach. It also is one of the nesting sites for for leatherback sea turtles. The main nesting season for local sea turtles is from March to September annually.

Caroni Swamp

Along the 12,000-acre Caroni Swamp and Bird Sanctuary you can find the Scarlet Ibis bird. This is one of our national birds, along with the Cocrico, the only game bird native to Tobago and is not found in Trinidad. It is the perfect location for bird watching. These mangroves provide perfect resting places for these mangroves. On these boat tours you get to see the 100 plus species of other birds, caimans and crabs of different species that nest there.

Asa Wright Nature Centre & Lodge

This is a non-profit organization established in 1967. It comprises nearly 1,500 acres of mainly forested land in the Arima and Aripo Valleys. It aims to protect the wildlife habitat.This is perfect place for all bird watchers. There are about 166 species of birds seen on the centre's grounds. One of the most spectacular sites for visitors is the nesting of the Crested Oropendola colony.

Book tour now


Mount St. Benedict

You can tour the Mount St. Benedict, a community of monks living, praying, and working together. It was founded in October 1912 by an order of monks from Brazil. They are famous for their yogurt,local sweets, jams and jellies from fruits grown on the mountain top (600 Acres). Enjoy the wonderful atmosphere, and beautiful mountainous view.

Book tour now


La Brea Pitch Lake

This is located on Trinidad’s south-western coast.The Spanish called it ’Tierra de Brea’, meaning land of pitch and the name eventually became La Brea. It holds approximately 10 million tonnes of asphalt, one of the largest deposits of natural asphalt in the world. The lake spans some 109 acres and has been named 'The eighth wonder of the world by villagers. Visitors can carefully walk on its surface and may hear some burping noises.
